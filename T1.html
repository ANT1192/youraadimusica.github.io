Tabla

Tabla is a percussion instruement from north of India. It has pair of drums, one is traditionally known as baayan(left) i.e., base drum and other one is tabla that has a varity of sounds. Generally the left drum is made up of a heavy metal like brass ,copper etc. Heavier the base drum great is the sound and tabla is made of a solid wood. The two are covered with goat skin in order to resonate on hitting by tabla pair to produce required sound.

Tabla is tuned with help of a metal hammer at different scales. Tabla is basically an accompnyning instruement but solo tabla playing has it's own charm and legacy. Tabla is played with Sarod, Sitar, classical dance, santoor, flute and playback singers in accompaniment .

Tabla is played in hindustani classical. It is played with north indian traditional "Thumarie" , "Gazal", "Bhajan", "Sufi", "Quawali" etc.The tabla is also an important instrument in the bhakti devotional traditions of Hinduism and Sikhism, such as during bhajan and kirtan singing. The devlopment of masculer tradition in the hands make the hand of a tabla player worth play. It is generally played in various countries like India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka etc of Asia.

The playing technique is complex and involves extensive use of the fingers and palms in various configurations to create a wide variety of different sounds and rhythms, reflected in mnemonic syllables (bol).
